McCarthyism Defined

Senator Joseph McCarthy led a wave of anti-communist hysteria in the 1950s. His tactics included:

  • Accusations without evidence — naming alleged communists with no proof
  • Blacklisting — artists, writers, and professionals barred from work
  • Ruined lives — careers destroyed, families torn apart

HUAC (House Un-American Activities Committee) held hearings that turned suspicion into a weapon.

Mass Hysteria

Mass hysteria is the rapid spread of collective anxiety, rumors, or beliefs through a population. It fueled McCarthyism.

The 10 Proven Conspiracies

Declassified documents and investigations have confirmed these. Hover for details; click for sources.

MKUltra

CIA mind-control experiments with LSD on unwitting citizens. Ran for years; many files were destroyed.

Church Committee, CIA declassified docs. See footer.

COINTELPRO

FBI program to disrupt and discredit civil rights and activist groups, including Dr. King.

FBI COINTELPRO documents, Church Committee.

Tuskegee Syphilis Study

U.S. Public Health Service withheld treatment from Black men with syphilis for 40 years.

CDC, National Archives. Tuskegee University history.

Operation Paperclip

U.S. brought Nazi scientists to America after WWII, hiding their war crimes to secure their expertise.

U.S. government archives, Operation Paperclip by Annie Jacobsen.

Operation Northwoods

Pentagon planned fake terrorist attacks to justify war on Cuba. Rejected by JFK.

Declassified JCS documents, National Security Archive.

Nayirah Testimony

Kuwaiti ambassador's daughter lied to Congress about Iraqi atrocities to rally support for the Gulf War.

Congressional testimony, later investigations (e.g. Harper's).

Gulf of Tonkin Incident

Fabricated or exaggerated attack used to escalate U.S. involvement in the Vietnam War.

NSA declassified documents, Pentagon Papers.

The Business Plot

Businessmen allegedly tried to overthrow FDR with a fascist coup in 1933. Exposed by Smedley Butler.

Congressional investigation (McCormack-Dickstein), historical accounts.

Operation Mockingbird

CIA infiltrated and influenced American media for decades. Church Committee documented it.

Church Committee reports, CIA archives.

Operation Snow White

Scientologists infiltrated 136 U.S. government agencies to purge unfavorable records.

FBI files, court documents, St. Petersburg Times investigation.

How Hysteria Sells: Logical Fallacies in Rhetoric

Orators who profit from hysteria—through power, money, or influence—often rely on logical fallacies to bypass critical thinking.

Ad hominem

Attacking the person instead of the argument. “Don’t listen to them—they’re one of them / paid off / crazy.” Discredits the messenger so the audience never has to engage with evidence.

Appeal to emotion

Using fear, anger, or outrage instead of evidence. Makes you feel first, think second (or never). Keeps audiences in a state of alarm so they stop asking for proof.

Slippery slope

Claiming one step will inevitably lead to disaster. “If we allow X, then Y and Z will follow.” Creates a false sense of urgency and inevitability so you act before you think.

Survival Guide: Spot the Pattern

These fallacies work together: an orator identifies an enemy (ad hominem against critics), stokes fear (appeal to emotion), and warns that inaction means doom (slippery slope). The profit comes from attention, donations, book sales, or political power.

Red flags

  • Someone attacks people instead of addressing the argument or evidence.
  • Emotion is cranked up while evidence is downplayed or dismissed.
  • You’re told the sky is falling and you must act now—or it’s too late.

When you see these patterns: pause. Ask for evidence. Stay skeptical.
